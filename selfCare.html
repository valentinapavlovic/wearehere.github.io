Net Neutrality

All about net neutrality

Net neutrality is the idea that all internet should be treated equally and is not influenced by any data other than itself. This prevents companies from blocking certain websites or slowing internet speeds. Things that violate it include favoring private networks, discriminating against certain IP addresses, and favoring fast loading websites.

Why is it important?

Net neutrality is important because if we didn’t have it we would likely have to pay for all of our internet usage rather than just a connection. Companies would be able to restrict access to sites you didn’t pay for, artificially slow some services, and filter out or block certain content. It would make the internet unaccessible for many.

Arguments for neutrality

The arguments for neutrality are mainly that it preserves digital rights and freedom and allows for competition and innovation. In the US, net neutrality is somewhat considered a right because it protects freedom of speech. The end to end principle argues that net neutrality is necessary for the internet to remain a source of social and economic good, which encompasses the other arguments mentioned.
